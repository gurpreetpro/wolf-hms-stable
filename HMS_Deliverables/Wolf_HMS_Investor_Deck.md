## 1. The Market Opportunity: "The Fragmented Hospital"

### The Problem
Today's "Gold Standard" hospitals are bleeding revenue due to **Digital Fragmentation**. A typical Tier-1 hospital pays for 5-7 disconnected software stacks:

1.  **EMR/EHR (Epic/Cerner)**: \$10M+ implementation. Great at records, poor at UX and interoperability.
2.  **Physical Security (Lenel/Tyco)**: Separate silo. Guards don't know who is a patient or a threat.
3.  **Revenue Cycle (RCM)**: Separate clearinghouses and manual coding teams.
4.  **Patient Engagement**: 3rd party apps for telemedicine.
5.  **Analytics**: Expensive BI tools (Tableau/PowerBI) wired on top.

### The Cost of Fragmentation
*   **Data Silos**: Security doesn't talk to Clinical. A "Code Violet" (Violence) in the ER doesn't automatically lock down the specific wing in the EMR.
*   **Vendor Fatigue**: maintenance contracts for 5 vendors.
*   **Slow Innovation**: Legacy codebase (MUMPS/Java) takes years to update.

## 4. The "Secret Sauce": Technical Moat

Our audit of the Wolf codebase reveals three "Unfair Advantages" that are hard to copy:

### Moat #1: The Security-Clinical Bridge
*   *Code Evidence*: `securityController.js` listens to `systemBus`.
*   *Scenario*: a "Panic Button" press (SOS) triggers a `CODE_VIOLET`. The system can instantly lock doors (`toggleLockdown`) AND alert nursing staff in the EMR.
*   *Competitors*: Impossible. Their EMR doesn't talk to the door locks.

### Moat #2: Native AI Core
*   *Code Evidence*: `ICD10Coder.jsx` and `AIClinicalSummary`.
*   *Scenario*: Codes are suggested *as the doctor speaks/types*.
*   *Competitors*: Require expensive 3rd party plugins (Nuance/3M) to do this. Wolf builds it in *free*.

### Moat #3: Simulation-First Engineering
*   *Code Evidence*: `simulation_atomic.js`.
*   *Scenario*: Wolf allows hospitals to "Simulate" a busy day (10k patients) to test workflows *before* go-live.
*   *Competitors*: "Test in Production" or manual QA.
